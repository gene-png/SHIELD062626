## Lessons learned (Sprint 2)

- **De-brittle before you re-seed.** The 8 hardcoded-UUID specs only surfaced as
  broken once the local image/volume cache was empty at launch and the DB
  reseeded with fresh ids — hence T0 was reordered to depend on T1. Runtime id
  resolution (`ids.ts`) is now the contract; a repo grep enforces zero
  hardcoded UUIDs under `e2e/`.
- **Reuse the framework's own validation, don't hand-roll.** The reserved-TLD
  guard (T9) reuses email-validator's `SPECIAL_USE_DOMAIN_NAMES` check via a
  probe — the exact check `EmailStr` runs at registration — so approval and
  registration can never drift.
- **Fix at the point of the mistake, fail loudly.** `beacon.test` was
  approvable-but-unregistrable; rejecting at approval time (not silently later
  at registration) is the fail-loud posture. Same spirit as the CSF draft guard
  returning the existing draft instead of minting silently.
- **a11y focus vs. auto-save.** Roving-tabindex that auto-selects on arrow would
  flood the auto-save PATCH; decoupling focus from selection (select on
  Space/Enter/click) keeps both the a11y contract and the data plane sane.
- **Verbatim source of record:** the CSF interview prompts are the
  `app/csf/catalog.py` `SUBCATEGORIES` (NIST CSF 2.0 Final text), NOT the master
  spec `.txt` (which defines only the subcategory model) — documented in the s3
  spec + SMOKE_TEST §3.
- **CI honesty:** the new e2e job's first real run is pending the
  review-required push; its step block was proven locally against
  `e2e/README.md`, and that limitation is stated in the commit body — not
  claimed as green.
